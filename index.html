The Book

A wedding gift.
Bavaria, 1956.

Erna Paul received this cookbook on the day she got married.
It is written in old Fraktur typeface — the script of a world
that no longer exists.

The book is called Bayerisches Kochbuch — Bavarian Cookbook. Maria Hofmann wrote it. It went through twenty-six editions. Erna received the twenty-sixth, bound in cloth, the year West Germany was still rebuilding.

She's gone now. But the book is still here. And so are the recipes.

Babette's Table is named for Babette Martin — Erna's grandmother, the steady, loving presence who held the family together through genuinely difficult years. Every recipe in this collection carries both their names.

We translate each recipe by hand, adapt it for a modern American kitchen, and add the story behind it: what Bavaria looked like in 1956, what it meant to cook from scratch in the years after the war, and who the women were who kept these kitchens running.
